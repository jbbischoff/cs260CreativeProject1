Class Rules

In my classroom we will follow all Renaissance Academy school rules. These rules are simple and effective. I am dedicated to their enforcement in order to facilitate the best possible learning environment for every student. These rules are meant to create and foster respect, cooperation, and a safe learning environment for all students. The rules and consequences are as follows:

Rules

  • We are Successful: We come prepared, learn from our mistakes, and always try our best.

  • We are Respectful: To others, teacher, self, and property.

  • We are Responsible: We arrive on-time and are organized.

  • We are Honest: We tell the truth and do our own work (unless it’s group work).

  • We are Kind: We think before we speak, and are positive.

  • We are Safe: We keep our hands to ourselves and only use objects for their intended purpose.

Consequences

  1. Warning.

  2. In-classroom time-out and discussion with the teacher about their behavior.

  3. The student will be given a “Behavior Reflection Form” to fill out in class. This form may be sent home for a parent signature and is to be returned the following day.

  4. If poor behavior continues, the student will be sent home with an after-school detention form. This form is to be signed and returned the following day. Parents may also be notified with a phone call or email after school. Detention is held after school for 30 minutes.

  5. Any extreme bad behavior will result in the student being sent down to the office. Parents will be notified.

I dislike assigning detention. I don’t want to do it. But I am just as obligated to follow the class rules as my students are. I also have a zero-tolerance policy for any bullying behavior. Cell phones are never allowed in class.

I believe in clean slates, so every day we begin again from the top, regardless of consequences handed out the previous day. That being said, if a student earns multiple in-classroom time-outs within the same week, a “Behavior Reflection” will be filled out and the student may earn a detention.

Food and Drink Policy

Students are welcome to have a water bottle on their desk. Water bottle flipping will not be tolerated. In accordance with Renaissance Academy policy, snacks are not allowed during class. If students are hungry, they can eat at lunchtime or quickly eat a snack between classes.

Because we have students with severe nut allergies, my classroom is a nut-free zone. Students should leave any nutty snacks in their lockers as these will not be allowed in class.

Absences

It is the student’s responsibility to catch up on any material missed during an absence. This can be done by checking the my blog for assignments, sending me an email, or speaking with me after class. If advance notice is given, the student will be given a packet with work to complete. I prefer students to get their work done ahead of time so they won’t be lost when they return.

Sickness and Medication

If your child becomes sick during the day they will be allowed to call home from the phone in the office. They will still need to be checked out from the office and parents can pick them up there and follow proper check-out procedure. If your child has special medication needs during school hours, the medicine needs to be taken to the office and dispensed from there. This excludes things such as cough drops, etc.

Grading

At the heart of this policy is the belief that a grade should be an accurate measurement of what the student knows more than anything else as we move throughout the school year. This has far-reaching ramifications.

If the grade should represent what a student knows, then padding a grade with extra credit assignments is not appropriate because the amount, type, and worth of these assignments may vary from year to year, teacher to teacher. Not only that, extra credit shows how hard a student is willing to work (usually last minute) to make up for earlier failings. Again, this does not necessarily represent what a child knows. It just shows how much he or she can work—which is commendable, but it doesn’t help teachers gauge where the student is at in terms of understanding required material. Extra credit work only serves to pad a poor grade and does not reflect what a student actually knows. Thus, extra credit will not be given and attendance and citizenship are also not counted in the main grade.

There are two types of assessments/assignments. Formative and Summative:

  • Formative assessments are limited to a maximum of 25% of a child’s grade. These assignments are things such as daily work, group work, and homework. They help me measure how a student is progressing and allow me to tell how much time I need to dedicate to teaching a principle.

  • Summative assessments are things like quizzes, tests, and major writing assignments. They are worth a minimum of 75% of a student’s grade. They measure actual attainment of skills and knowledge. If your child is absent on assessment days or does not turn in major writing assignment on time, their grade may suffer greatly.

The academic proficiency scale is as follows:

  1. A (90-100%) Advanced / Exceeds Standards

  2. B (80-89%) Proficient / Meets Standards

  3. C (70-79%) Nearing Proficiency / Approaching Standards

  4. D (<69%) Novice / Below Standards

Late work will be accepted, but points will be docked based on how late the work is. Until the late work is submitted, a zero will appear in the gradebook. This is a place holder. If I could enter an ‘I’ for incomplete, I would. I want every student to complete their assignments so that they can demonstrate mastery of the content before they are tested. That said, if I receive a request for 20 missing assignments right before the end of term, I will make an exception to this policy and NOT accept that work. Not doing homework during an entire term and then submitting it all at the end does not help me accurately measure a student’s progress through the year. Late work, if it is necessary, should be submitted within a month of the due date. One week before the term end, late work of any kind will no longer be accepted.

I allow my students to retake tests or quizzes if they scored a C or lower. There are two ways to do this: students can choose to either fix their incorrect answers for partial points, or they can retake the test in its entirety for full points. This must be completed in my classroom, outside of regular class time. Students may come in before school, during lunch, during recess, or after school, and I will make the test available to them. Tests remain “open” for three weeks, during which time students may correct their answers or retake the test. Three weeks after the test was originally administered, the test closes and corrections are no longer possible.

If you have any questions regarding these policies, please email me at sambischoff@notarealemail.com and I will do my best to answer promptly.

Homework

I will do my best to assign as little homework as possible. That said, when I do assign homework, I expect it to be completed. I will avoid scheduling homework over weekends and holidays. The exception to this rule is personal reading and the occasional large assignment. In these cases, I will give ample time to complete the assignment on week nights if the student so chooses. This is so that with hard work and planning, the assignment can be completed in school or on weeknights. With grammar and spelling worksheets, homework will only be assigned if I truly believe the students need extra practice in order to grasp a principle.

Any missing assignments must be completed. Missing work is not acceptable. In my classroom I make information about missing assignments readily available so that my students are aware of what they are missing. I may send out an email during the term informing parents about missing work. My intent with these emails is purely informative. I want you to be involved with your child’s education and to know if they are completing their work or falling behind, but I also believe that keeping on top of assignments is primarily the responsibility of the student.

Assessments

Assessments will be administered periodically. Students may retake tests for full points. Half points will be given for every “answer correction.” This gives the student the opportunity to turn tests into learning experiences as well as give every student the opportunity to score better on the exam. For example, correcting answers instead of retaking the test can turn a grade of 60/100 (D-) into 80/100 (B-). It’s worth the extra work!

Book Reports

Book reports will be due about every six weeks. Each student’s independent reading will work in tandem with this assignment. Students must complete 280 minutes of reading every two weeks – about 20 minutes a day. Independent reading can be tracked with a reading log bookmark. At the end of every two weeks this bookmark will be turned in and given a grade based on submission rather than completion. This will allow me to better track student reading habits. I believe that solo reading at home is the single biggest indicator for success. Consistent reading will improve all aspects of a student’s education. This is very important.

I am giving students the option to select their own texts. I also have a list of recommended titles if they are out of ideas. So if a student hates what they read it’s their own fault! The report shouldn’t be a huge project. It can take many different shapes, so make it fun! I am mostly interested in having the students read on a regular basis. I will include a list of ideas for the book report assignment on the website under “Resources”.

Major Writing Assignments

Essays will be a large part of this class. This is usually a paper based on a theme or a text we have read together. We will take time in class to work on structure, flow, and mechanics to prepare students to write essays of increasing length. These will make up a significant part of a student’s grade because this is how I assess a student’s writing ability. The entire rough draft should be completed in class. The only thing that may be done at home is typing and printing or submitting the final draft. If students are complaining about how much work they have to do at home on the papers, it is because they are not using class time wisely.

Final Research Paper

State standards require students to write at least one research-based paper this year. All of my teaching will be geared toward helping students write a cohesive, well-worded 3 page paper. This paper will be assigned near the end of the year. It will be based off of a self-generated question. By the time the final paper rolls around, the students will be little experts in writing papers. There is no need to feel daunted by this assignment.
