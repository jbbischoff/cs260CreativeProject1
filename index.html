Half-Way There

As Term 2 comes to a close, I would like to review some important classroom policies.

Tests and Quizzes close after 3 weeks. Missed tests must be taken within that time. Test retakes or test corrections must also be completed within that 3 week window. Once a test is closed, the grades entered are final. Exceptions can be made for extended absences that have been discussed with me beforehand.

Formative assignments may be turned in late with no point penalty up until my late work deadline for each term (usually one week before the term ends).

Summative assignments may be turned in late, but will have a point penalty that increases with each late day until the maximum grade for turning it in becomes an 80%. For this reason, it is imperative that summative assignments are turned in on time. (These assignments include final papers and book reports.)

The names of students missing tests is posted in my classroom. I also keep a list of missing assignments for each student. Both these lists are updated at least once weekly. I try to make this information as available as possible so that students can keep on top of their tests and assignments.

I am always happy to work with and assist students who approach me with questions or concerns. I am available for 20 minutes before school, during lunch or recess, or for 30 minutes after school (with occasional exceptions for meetings).

December 4, 2019

Test Correction/Retake Policy Change

This new reading curriculum has been really fun to dive into with my students, but new systems always come with a bug or two. It has come to my attention that when students review their online tests, it not only tells them the wrong answer, but tells them which answer was correct. This makes it impossible for students to correct their wrong answers for partial points or to retake tests.

It is important to me that students are given opportunities to learn from their mistakes and have second chances to demonstrate their knowledge. Test retakes and corrections are a great learning opportunity for the students who take advantage of them and also prepares students for future tests and assessments. For these reasons, I have made a few adjustments to my test correction and retake policy, as well as to the way we take tests in class.

We will no longer be taking tests on the myngconnect system, though we may still use computers at times.

Tests will remain open for three weeks, during which time students may make answer corrections or retake the test. After the three weeks, the test closes and changes can no longer be made to the grade.

Test retakes and corrections must be done in my classroom either before school, during lunch or recess, or after school.

One Month Down….

The first month of Middle School can always be challenging. Students are trying to get used to their schedules, and having so many teachers. Organization is a constant struggle, and students are learning how to take accountability for their own success.

It can be a rough transition period, and I do my best as a teacher to help students learn successful habits.

In my class all students should have a three ring binder and a folder (in addition to lined paper and pencils). That means that every student should have at least two if not four “pockets” to put work. In class, to help struggling students with their organization, I have encouraged them to designate one of their pockets for the following purposes:

  • Work that is not done yet and needs to be finished

  • Work that is done and needs to be turned in

  • Work that has been graded and passed back

On my white board I keep an accurate assignment list, outlining that will be due at the end of a given week. Students are encouraged to write these lists into their planners so that they can keep track of what work is expected of them that week.

I keep a list of missing work for each student in a packet in my classroom as well. This is updated weekly. Students know that they can check the packet to see what assignments or tests they are missing.

I also allow test makeups if a student was absent or received a low score. Tests are only open for two weeks (unless a student has been absent for an extended time). This means that all test makeups must be completed within that time frame. Students can choose to either retake the test for full points or fix their incorrect answers for partial points.

It is important to me that my students learn accountability and gain the skills that will make them successful lifelong learners.

It’s going to be a great year!
